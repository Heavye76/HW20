Omni Youth Programs
Omni Youth Programs is a non-profit dedicated to creating strong families, develop youth leaders and engage the community to prevent youth alcohol, marijuana and other drug use.  Omni Youth offers training programs and resources to foster communication between parents and their at-risk-teens. Our intent was to redesign their website to provide a simple and effortless user experience.

Proto Persona
I’ve always approached a proto-persona as an opportunity for our stakeholder (or in this case myself) to lay all of our assumptions on the table. Once we declare to the world what defines our user, we will work our way through the design thinking process to gain a better understanding of our users’ behaviors, needs, and motivations. Insights gained from this process will validate or invalidate the various aspects of our proto-persona. By the end of this process our proto-persona will evolve into our user persona.

User Research
Seven (7) user interviews were conducted by Zoom, phone & in-person.  Qualitative data from the interviews revealed that concerned parents want to open the lines of communication with their at-risk-teens, but often times they aren’t sure how. Parents lose sleep worrying about peer pressure and gateway drugs. They don’t know where to find help and are unaware of local programs that could help. In the end, frustrated parents fall back on searching the internet.

Design With Intent - Observe & Apply
Card Sorting / Sitemap
The card sorting exercise has always been one of my favorites, it’s how you start to mold the structure of the website. 3x5 cards, a sharpie and a kitchen table were used to restructure and organize the content in a meaningful matter. The goal is to provide a logical, clear structure for our users so that it’s easier for them to navigate and access information. Bottom line, the user can find what they’re looking for with the least amount of effort.

Heuristics
When I first started design, I figured that in a redesign project, that you go scorched earth and completely rebuild everything. That’s about 75% of the gig. That other 25% comes from reviewing and evaluating the heuristics of the current design. It gives the designer the opportunity to see what worked and what didn’t.
  • The contrast is overwhelming - The colors add on to the level of chaos.
  • Navigation Bar – is crowded with 9 items, many of them could be easily be put into the footer.
  • Complete lack of balance, proximity, and alignment.
  • Whitespace – There is white space, but it’s not used in an efficient manner.
Designing with Intent
A part of the design process no one ever mentions is that as you work your way through a redesign project you really get to know the website/organization, intimately. You find components they did really well, and others that need to go. In this case, the content was solid but the interface was a dumpster fire. Following the heuristic evaluation I wanted to fall back on some of the fundamental UI design principles. The architecture is based on the following:
  • Balance – Use visual weight to create relationship between elements
  • Proximity – Group together related design elements
  • Alignment – Build order, organization and improve readability
  • Whitespace – Maximize whitespace to bring clarity and focus to the design.

Usability Testing Rounds 1 & 2
We wanted to see if our prototype was organized appropriately and if the content on each page was easily accessible. We were also looking to see if the average web user is able to navigate through the site with minimal issues. Wireframes were iterated following each round of usability tests.
Round 1
6 Tests
  • Headers are not uniform size and color
  • Repositioned Navigation Bar
  • Moved mission statement to float over Hero Image
  • Information Cards are different sizes and shapes
  • Margins and spacing are inconsistent
  • Donate button looks like a scam
Round 2
4 Tests
  • Text is too small on Homepage
  • Mission statement on hero image does not match the one on About Us
  • Program Titles need contrast for the users to read
  • The "Active Parenting of Teens" rollover doesn’t work properly
  • Too much plain text and whitespace
  • Remove old logo in Footer
  • Blog section text is too small

Final Thoughts
A website’s usability is a very important element in giving the user a good first impression.

Seeing a non-profit organization implement such an ineffective and weak UI made us wonder how many people have overlooked Omni Youth Program’s website due to poor design and functionality.

This redesign attempts to correct the original design flaws and help the visitor obtain information about the organization in a simple and easy way.
